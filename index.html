Ladi Kwali

A contemporary Nigerian artist.

About the Legend

☛ Ladi was born in the small village of Kwali, present Kwali Area Council of the Federal Capital Territory, in 1925 (Other historians indicate her date of birth is actually 1920) and raised in a family that kept up with the folkloric female tradition of pottery making.

☛ From the early 1934, she learned to make pottery as a child from her aunt using the traditional method of coiling.

☛ In 1954, Ladi joined the Abuja Pottery Training Centre, with the invitation of Michael Cardew (pottery officer in the Department of Commerce and Industry). She became the Centre’s first female potter.

☛ In 1958, Ladi Kwali’s pots were featured in the international exhibition of Abuja pottery organized by Cardew in London.

☛ In 1959, Kwali’s work was shown to great acclaim in London at the Berkeley Galleries.

☛ In 1960, her works were displayed at the Nigerian Independence Exhibition during the Independence Day celebration.

☛ In 1963, Ladi Kwali was awarded an MBE (Member of the Most Excellent Order of the British Empire). She conducted a series of workshops and exhibitions in Germany, Italy, and Switzerland.

☛ Ladi Kwali conducted series of workshops in Chicago, Dallas, Los Angeles, and other major cities in the US. She received the Silver Award for Excellence, Tenth International Exhibit of ceramic Art, Smithsonian Institution, Washington DC all between 1964 - 1965.

☛ She was awarded a honorary doctorate by the Ahmadu Bello University, Zaria after she spent a session at the University giving classes on her pottery-making techniques in 1977.

☛ In 1980 the Nigerian Government invested in Kwali the insignia of the Nigerian National Order of Merit Award (NNOM), the highest national honour for academic achievement. The Abuja Pottery Centre was renamed the Ladi Kwali Pottery Centre.

☛ In 1981, Ladi Kwali received the National Honour of the Officer of the Order of the Niger (OON). A major street was named Ladi Kwali Road in Abuja. Sheraton Hotel housed the Ladi Kwali Convention Center, one of the largest conference facilities in Abuja with ten meeting rooms and 4 ballrooms.

☛ On 12th August 1984, Hadiza Ladi Kwali died in Minna, Niger State, Nigeria.
